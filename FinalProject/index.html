A Brief Intro of Chinese Hotpot!

Location: Beijing, China

Soupbase:

Long-hour stewed pork-bone soup with slices of ginger, minced green onion, mushrooms, goji berry and dates

What is known for:

Bejing hotpot is known for its thin-sliced, hand crafted lamb meat. Every slice is controlled in 0.9mm to 1.2mm, which is thin enough for the light to go through. The typical three vegeterian choices for the hotpot are cabbage, tofu and glass noodle.

Recommended Sauce:

Sugar pickled garlic, minced leek, sesame paste and fermented tofu juice

Location: Chongqing, China

Soupbase:

Ingredients include: thick layers of beef tallow, Sichuan peper, chilli. The soup is usually burning spicy.

What is known for:

The middle grid is for fast boils such as animal haslet (must haves), which only needs 8-15 seconds. The cross grids are for food that needs to be boiled for 2-10 minutes. Lastly, the four corners are for stewing over 20 minutes. The tradition is to boil meat before vegetables.

Location: Sichuan, China

Soupbase:

Ingredients include: fermented soy beans, chilli, Sichuan peper, ginger, amazake, salt,chicken powder, and many other spices. The soup is usually burning spicy.

What is known for:

Put raw skewers into the soup and boils for 5-10 minutes. It's known for the variety of ingredients and the easiness to eat.

Recommended Sauce:

Oil dish: sesame oil, mashed garlic, salt, minced parsley and green onion, millet pepper. Dry dish: chilli powder, peparika powder, aginomotoï¼Œpeanut crunches. Must-have is the sprinkle of sesame on top of the ingredients.

Location: Coastal area of China

Soupbase:

Ingredients include: Pork bone soup, salt, lapa and Shaoxing rice cooking wine. The soup is usually very savory and drinkable.

What is known for:

The seafood hotpot is known for its freshness of the seafood ingredients. The hotopot usually includes crab, shrimp, fish, clams, cabbage, mushroom, frozen bean curd, and glass noodle.

Recommended Sauce:

Vinegar, chopped garlic, sliced ginger and a little bit soy sauce. Sometimes, couple drops of lime.

Location: Guangdong, China

Soupbase:

Ingredients include: depends on the preference, some people choose to use spring water, but more traditional soupbase includes beef bone soup and galangal. The soup is usually clear.

What is known for:

Chaoshan beef hotpot is known for its freshness of the beef and chef's adept slicing skills. The beef is usually served within 2-3 hours fresh window, and they served by different parts of the cow. The best way to eat is a quick dip of 5-10 seconds of a slice of beef.

Recommended Sauce:

Satay dish: peanuts, sesame, dried sea shrimp, fermented soy bean, fried garlic and chopped green onion.

Location: Hainan, China

Soupbase:

Ingredients include: mixed young and old coconut, a spring chicken, rice cooking wine, soy sauce, ginger and salt. The soup is usually savory and a little bit sweet.

What is known for:

Coconut chicken hotpot is known for its light taste. Unlike other hotpot, this hotpot is not greasy or strong. It tastes the best with cabbage, sweet corn, and dried bamboo fungus.

Recommended Sauce:

Minced galangal, chopped chilli, soy sauce and drops of lime juice.
